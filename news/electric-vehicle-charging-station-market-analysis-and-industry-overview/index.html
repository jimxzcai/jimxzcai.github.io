Electric Vehicle Charging Station Market Analysis and Industry Overview

Electric Vehicle (EV) Charging Station Market Analysis

The electric vehicle (EV) charging station market is projected to reach $328.6 billion by 2024 and is expected to reach $1040.9 billion by 2029, with a compound annual growth rate of 25.94% during the forecast period (2024-2029). The COVID-19 pandemic forced around 95% of automotive-related companies to halt their operations during the lockdown period. The impact of the lockdown was significant and unprecedented globally due to the cessation of manufacturing activities. However, with the recovery of global economic activities and an increase in automobile production, the market regained momentum. Significant growth is anticipated in the market over the next five years as the economy gradually returns to normalcy.

Long-term growth in the electric vehicle charging infrastructure can be attributed to the implementation of stringent emission and fuel economy standards, government incentives, and increasing sales of electric vehicles, all of which generate demand for charging stations. Some notable companies are also investing in the development of electric vehicle charging stations. For example, in October 2022, Octopus Energy Generation made its first investment in the UK’s electric vehicle charging infrastructure, planning to invest up to £110 million in Be.EV, a public electric vehicle charging network based in Manchester. The agreement aims to expand Be.EV’s network of 150 public charging points. EV has committed to adding 1,000 charging stations in northern England and other regions.

The electric vehicle charging station market is witnessing various new technologies expected to hit the market in the coming years. Participants in the market are researching technologies such as wireless charging and autonomous charging robots, which could make vehicle charging more convenient. For instance, in January 2021, Siemens AG launched a new high-power charger, Sicharge D, featuring scalable high charging power of up to 300 kW. The charging station also supports voltages from 150 to 1,000 volts and charging currents of up to 1,000.

Europe and North America are expected to dominate the market, followed by the Asia-Pacific region. Growth in this region is driven by electric vehicle sales and production, as well as the penetration of electric vehicles and commercial vehicles in major countries over the next few years.

Public Charging Stations Leading the Electric Vehicle Charging Station Market

The availability of public electric vehicle charging stations is crucial for electric vehicle adoption worldwide. The ability to charge quickly at public charging stations is considered a key criterion when purchasing electric vehicles, expected to drive revenue growth in the public charging sector. As the number of electric vehicle users continues to increase, the Asia-Pacific region continues to rapidly install public charging stations, especially in China, India, and South Korea. For example, in October 2022, Ather Energy announced the installation of its 580th public fast-charging point, Ather Grid, in 56 cities in India. The company plans to install an additional 820 grids, bringing the total to 1400 by the end of the 2023 fiscal year.

These countries are implementing policies to encourage electric vehicle adoption by providing subsidies, reducing taxes, and offering incentives for electric vehicle-related companies to expand more rapidly, thereby promoting the development of electric vehicle manufacturers and related industries. Economic growth, urbanization, steady growth in travel demand, and increased investment in electric vehicles for energy storage and environmental sustainability are expected to drive growth in the public charging station sector. Governments worldwide have introduced various plans and initiatives to encourage buyers to choose electric vehicles over traditional vehicles. The California ZEV program is one such initiative, aiming to put 1.5 million electric vehicles on the road by 2025. Countries like India, China, the UK, South Korea, France, Germany, Norway, and the Netherlands offer various incentives for those interested in purchasing electric vehicles. These developments and factors are expected to contribute to the growth of the public charging station sector.

The Asia-Pacific Region Likely to Play a Key Role in the Market

In the Asia-Pacific region, China is the largest market for electric vehicles and public buses. The Chinese electric vehicle charging station market is supported by the growing market for electric vehicles and strong government support. China has expanded incentives for purchasing new energy vehicles (NEVs). According to data released by the China Electric Vehicle Charging Infrastructure Promotion Alliance in August 2020, as of July 2020, there were approximately 566,000 public charging stations installed and operational nationwide, including 326,000 alternating current (AC) charging stations, 240,000 direct current (DC) charging stations, and 488 stations with both AC and DC capabilities. In July 2020, the total electricity consumption of all charging stations nationwide reached 670 million kilowatt-hours, a year-on-year increase of 52.4%.

As demand for zero-emission vehicles increases, Japan’s electric vehicle market is experiencing growth. The government is also heavily investing in the electric vehicle market. Japan aims to transition all new vehicles sold domestically to electric or hybrid vehicles by 2050. The government has also set targets to reduce carbon dioxide emissions and other greenhouse gas emissions per vehicle by around 80% by 2050. In addition, private sector companies are taking proactive steps and forming strategic partnerships to develop charging infrastructure. For example, in November 2022, PT PLN (Persero) signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) with PT Industri Ion Mobilitas at the Jakarta Convention Center (JCC) to accelerate the use of battery-based electric vehicles (KBLBB) in Indonesia. This collaboration will begin with 100 public charging stations (SPLU) in Jakarta.

These developments and initiatives are expected to drive demand for electric vehicle charging stations in the coming years.

Overview of the Electric Vehicle (EV) Charging Station Industry

The electric vehicle charging station market is fairly consolidated, dominated by a few companies such as State Grid Corporation of China, ABB, Siemens, Qingdao TGOOD Electric Co., Ltd., and Tesla, among others. Some participants are collaborating with governments to develop charging infrastructure. For instance, in November 2022, Yulu signed a memorandum of understanding with the Karnataka government and announced plans to invest ₹12 billion to deploy a fleet of 100,000 electric vehicles (EVs) and operate the largest electric vehicle battery charging and swapping infrastructure in the state nationwide over the next five years. In October 2022, the Luxembourg government announced the selection of 29 projects after the first call for companies to invest in electric vehicle charging infrastructure projects, offering financial assistance. Companies involved in building charging capacity of at least 175 kilowatts per station will receive up to 50% subsidy.

Furthermore, companies are forming strategic partnerships with other participants in charging infrastructure and charging stations. For example, in April 2021, Siemens Limited signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) with Switch Mobility Automotive Limited, a subsidiary of the Hinduja Group, to enter the electric commercial vehicle sector in India. In March 2021, BP joined forces with BMW Group and Daimler Mobility, becoming a digital charging solutions partner to drive electrification. BP will partner with BMW Group and Daimler Mobility in the field of digital charging solutions, where DCS is one of Europe’s leading developers of digital charging solutions for automotive manufacturers and fleet operators.
